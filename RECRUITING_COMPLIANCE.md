# Recruiting Compliance Guide - 2026

## California Requirements (KEY - MUST FOLLOW)

### Pay Transparency Law (SB 642)
- **REQUIRED:** Include salary range in ALL job postings if you have 15+ employees
- **Definition:** "Good faith estimate of salary or hourly wage range reasonably expected to pay upon hire"
- **Includes:** All forms of compensation (salary, bonuses, stock, benefits, etc.)
- **Penalty:** Fines and legal liability for non-compliance

### Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA)
- Cannot discriminate based on protected characteristics
- Must provide reasonable accommodations during hiring process
- Job requirements must be "essential functions" only

## Federal Requirements (ADA/EEOC)

### ADA Compliance
- Job postings must be accessible (screen reader compatible)
- Interview locations must be accessible
- Cannot ask about disabilities during initial screening
- Focus on "essential job functions" only

### EEOC Guidelines  
- Avoid discriminatory language (age, gender, etc.)
- Use neutral, inclusive language
- Document job requirements as business necessities

## Safe Language Guidelines

### AVOID:
- "Young," "energetic," "digital native" (age discrimination)
- "He/him" or gender-specific pronouns
- Physical requirements unless essential
- "Recent graduate" (age discrimination)

### USE:
- "Qualified candidate"
- "They/them" or neutral pronouns  
- "Able to perform essential functions with or without accommodation"
- Focus on skills and experience levels

## Job Posting Checklist
- [ ] Salary range included (CA requirement)
- [ ] Essential functions clearly defined
- [ ] Inclusive, neutral language used
- [ ] ADA accommodation statement included
- [ ] Equal opportunity employer statement
- [ ] No discriminatory requirements

## Standard Legal Disclaimers
**Equal Opportunity:** "We are an equal opportunity employer committed to diversity and inclusion."

**ADA:** "We provide reasonable accommodations for qualified individuals with disabilities in the application process."
